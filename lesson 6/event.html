MASS COMMUNICATION

 

COMING TO THE UNITED STATES

Introduction
Following the Nigerian government's failure to keep its commitment to pay all outstanding salaries and bonuses, the Academic Staff Union of Universities in Nigeria began an indefinite strike action. This inspired me to hunt for a different study location. In addition, I had always wanted to study abroad, so it was a no-brainer during my search that I would choose a university in the US to complete my bachelor's degree.

Obtaining a Visa
Everyone has experienced the disappointment that comes with rejection at least once in their life. The ongoing sense of unworthiness and uncertainty about one's real level of sufficiency.  It takes a lot out of you, both physically and mentally to get a visa. On August 11, 2022, I attended my first interview for a US visa. Never in my life have I been that anxious about anything else. To appropriately prepare for the interview, I participated in a number of practice sessions and viewed a number of internet videos, but to no avail. I arrived for my interview, and in less than 5 minutes, I was walking out with a letter of rejection. The days and weeks that followed were some of my hardest. Midway through responding to a question posed to me by the visa officer, I froze. Just a straightforward "why did you choose this university and course?" question, nothing complicated. I stopped speaking mid-sentence because of extreme nervousness and just stared into nothing. Days after, as I continued to duel on my failed interview, I kept asking myself what I ought to have done better and how I ought to have approached the interview to gain a different outcome.

“The one who falls and gets up is stronger than the one who never tried. Do not fear failure but rather fear not trying”- 

Roy T Bennett  

Coming to the United States
I chose to reapply after the first attempt ended in failure. This time, I entered with more assurance and less anxiety. I had a lot of faith in my abilities and responses. On my second attempt, my visa was approved, and I experienced a surreal feeling. The process of getting ready to go for the US from Nigeria got under way, and it was filled with a variety of emotions. Fear of the unknown in a strange country with no one to rely on but myself, extreme weathers and my dread of failing or getting lost. During my 27-hour flight, all of these emotions were completely replaced by excitement for the adventure and journey that lay ahead. I'm finally in America, and I'm having a terrific time.

 "Better a brain drain than a brain in the drain"- 

Rajiv Gandhi
